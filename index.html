Goal shifts your maintenance calories (what keeps your weight stable) up or down.
  • Aggressive fat loss (−25%) — fast, but only for a few weeks. Risks muscle loss and low energy.
  • Fat loss (−20%) — a standard cut, around 0.5–0.75 kg a week.
  • Mild cut (−10%) — slow and comfortable. Best protects muscle and training quality.
  • Recomp / maintain (0%) — eat at maintenance. Build muscle while slowly losing fat.
  • Lean mass (+10%) — the classic lean bulk. Muscle with minimal fat.
  • Muscle gain (+15%) — faster gain, a bit more fat along the way.
  • Aggressive bulk (+20%) — maximum gain and the most fat. Hard-gainers only.
Enter a goal weight and I will pick the right goal and calorie adjustment to get you there, and estimate how long it will take. Roughly 7,700 kcal is 1 kg, so 0.5 kg a week needs about a 550 kcal daily gap. You can still override anything below.
Protein per kg of bodyweight. It builds and protects muscle and keeps you full. The sweet spot is 1.6–2.2 g/kg.
  • 1.6 — the minimum to fully support muscle growth.
  • 1.8 — a little insurance above the minimum.
  • 2.0 — the common serious-lifter target.
  • 2.2 — most protective of muscle when you are cutting.
  • 2.4 — no real extra muscle benefit. Mainly appetite control.
Fat as a share of your calories. It supports hormones and vitamin absorption. Whatever is left after protein and fat becomes your carbs, so less fat means more carbs to train on.
  • 20% — the practical floor. Maximum carbs, but going lower can affect hormones.
  • 25% — balanced, with plenty of room for carbs.
  • 30% — good if you feel better on more fat and fewer carbs.
  • 35% — lower-carb leaning. Filling, but the least fuel for training.
A manual nudge on top of your goal. Formulas only estimate your maintenance and everyone burns differently, so adjust based on what the scale actually does.
  • Nudge up (+150) if you are not gaining after 2–3 weeks on a bulk.
  • Nudge down (−150) if fat is climbing too fast, or to deepen a cut.
  • Leave at 0 until you have a couple of weeks of weigh-ins to go on.
